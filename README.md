# Casestudy

## 🍔Impact of Food Delivery Delay on Order Cancellation
## 📌Project Description

This case study analyzes how delivery delays impact order cancellations in a food delivery platform. Using operational and customer data, the dashboard identifies key factors such as delay time, partner performance, payment methods and customer ratings that influence cancellation behavior. The goal is to help businesses reduce cancellations and improve customer experience.

## ❗Business Problem
- High order cancellation rate impacting revenue
- Lack of visibility into delay-related issues
- Poor delivery partner performance tracking
- Inability to identify high-risk orders/customers

## 🎯Key Objectives
- Analyze relationship between delivery delay and cancellation rate
- Identify delay thresholds that significantly increase cancellations
- Evaluate delivery partner performance
- Understand impact of payment methods on cancellations
- Provide actionable insights to reduce revenue loss

## 📊Key Insights
- ⏱️ Orders with delay >30 mins show a sharp increase in cancellations
- 🚚 Lower delivery partner ratings are strongly linked with higher delays and cancellations
- 💳 COD orders show slightly higher cancellation tendency compared to prepaid
- ⭐ Customers giving low ratings (1–2) are more likely to cancel
- 🏪 Certain restaurants consistently contribute to high delay orders → operational issue
- 📅 Peak delays occur on specific days, indicating demand-supply imbalance

## 📌Conclusion

This analysis clearly shows that delivery delay is a major driver of order cancellations. By identifying delay thresholds, underperforming delivery partners and high-risk segments, businesses can take proactive steps to reduce cancellations and improve customer satisfaction.

## 🚀Recommendations 
- ⚡ Optimize delivery time by improving dispatch and routing
- 🚚 Monitor and retrain low-performing delivery partners
- 🎯 Introduce real-time delay alerts to customers
- 💰 Offer compensation/discounts for delayed orders
- 📊 Prioritize high-risk orders (based on delay prediction)
